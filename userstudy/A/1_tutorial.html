What's an ontology?

In computer science, an ontology is a specification of conceptualizations.
Ontologies are used to capture knowledge about some domain of interest.
An ontology describes the concepts in the domain and also the relationships that hold between those concepts.

In the example ontology below, concpets about pizza is shown as an indented list.

What's ontology mapping?

Ontologies are typically created by different authors. As a result, the concepts and structures in them will differ even if these ontologies describe the same domain of interest.
Ontology mapping generates correspondences between concepts belonging to different ontologies.

Mapped concepts means that they are equivalent in their meaning and can be used interchangably.
